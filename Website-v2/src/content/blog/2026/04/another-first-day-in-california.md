## Morning

I love mornings at Ayres ❤️ I slept really well last night. I did wake around 4am local time. I'm normally awake well before then back home. But I didn't go to bed until close to 11pm local. So 4am wasn't the ideal time to wake up...LOL I began to pray and fell back to sleep until 6am...a more appropriate time to be waking up...hehehe I felt well rested and started my day off as I usually do. I spent some time getting dressed and journaling. And a moment of prayer time. I didn't do any Bible study work. But I don't do that everyday like I do the journaling.

![Sunrise from Ayres](https://res.cloudinary.com/dh6izdwyd/image/upload/v1776532743/IMG_8441_l72pnq.jpg "Sunrise from Ayres")

I was able to enjoy the sunrise from my room. It was coming up over the mountains shortly after I woke. So I was able to see it before I did my full routine. It was the best way to wake up today 🤩 I was finished with my routine by 7am and made my way down for breakfast. I enjoyed every bit of it. The food, the coffee, the sounds over others beginning their day, the sound of the chef making more food, and the apple juice 🤭 I don't always have apple juice. But it has become a tradition here at Ayres with Cherie. So it's a must have for me on these trips ❤️ And I did some reading.

I've steamed my clothes for the weekend's Dapper Day events. I'm getting better at doing that...hehehe I'm so glad I discovered that steaming was better than ironing these clothes. For one, it's so much easier...LOL But it's also better on the materials, I think. I learned about this through California...LOL The first time was on my November 2024 trip. The Air BnB I had said they had an iron for me. But when I got there, it was a steamer. I gave it a try and WOW! So I bought one when I got home. But I also remembered an episode of *The Big Bang Theory* where Sheldon was steaming his cosplay outfit for ComiCon...hehehe The story also takes place in California. So, I give California full credit for how I steam my dapper attire now...LOL

Something that fascinates me as I reflect on these last 5 trips to California since I began coming in 2024 is the fact that it feels like *home* to me. Especially when I'm driving around. My only other time here before was around 2003. I recall having a similar feeling back then, in a way. But these past few years, I've really noticed it. I don't really know where I'm going without the Maps and GPS from my phone. But I always get this sense that I know where I am and where I am going. Oddly, I don't get stressed driving here...Orange and LA Counties in California of all places...LOL And when I interact with people, it's like I've always lived here. I was even asked for directions on one of my trips last year and I knew where to tell them to go...LOL I can best describe this feeling as a similar feeling when I travel back to Indiana. I just know where everything is and how to talk to people there. Or how I am when I'm at Disney. Except, this experience isn't exclusive to Disneyland Resort when I'm here visiting. And this is how I feel at Zen when I pop in each week. I don't get this feeling anywhere else where I live right now...LOL But it doesn't always feel like *home* when I visit Indiana because I still feel like a bit of an outcast back there. So, California has become a place that I get to go to and truly feel at *home*...hehehe Something to continue reflecting on...
