As I approach another travel weekend, I'm thinking about how much I've travel this Spring. I realized I've stayed in 4 different states. I live in Florida. So, that counts. I've traveled to California twice. I stayed the night in Alabama a few times. So I count that as well. And I was in Indiana; which is where I'm headed to again this weekend.

If you count the states I traveled through on my way to California and Indiana, that adds 3 more states for a total of 7. I was in Nevada to and from California. And I passed through Kentucky and Tennessee  a few times on my drives to and from Indiana.

I feel so Blessed because all of that travel was to be with my best friend and family. California and Indiana have become just as much my home as Florida is. None of this travel was for business. It was always to be with the people I love most. I'm so grateful to be able to travel as often as I have been. It's something I don't take for granted.

As of now, this weekend's trip is my last for a bit. I don't have any plans to travel in June. I may go up to Indiana in July. There is a possibility of a California trip in August. And I do plan to go to California in November. I never imagined I'd be doing anything like this a few years ago. It's exciting! I love being with everyone and the experiences I've been having my make heart all the more happier.

I do hope to travel to a few other places in the coming years as well. I think I've talked about trips to the Carolinas again. And to see a friend in Georgia. I've all but written off international travel, though. It's on the bucket list. But as long as I'm not alone, I probably won't ever actually do it. I don't have anyone who would want to join me anyway...hehehe

I think that's a good thing because there is so much of the USA to still see. A lot of what I've seen since my first trip to California to see Cherie in 2024 I would never have seen if it weren't for our friendship. There are places here in Florida I wouldn't have seen if my parents didn't come for visits a few times a year. And there are even places in Indiana I've only been to because it's now a place I visit instead of live in.

Also, there are many things I've gotten to see along my drives to and from Indiana these past few years that I would never have seen had I not traveled. There is plenty to add to a list of places to stop along that route someday...hehehe
